Why now

Today, we learn about an outbreak only after hospitals fill.

Clinical surveillance sees a person after they fall ill and seek care. By then a pathogen has already spread for weeks. Symptomatic-only testing and household surveys under-report what is really circulating in a community.

⏱ Today

Signals arrive late — after cases reach clinics and hospitals. Response is reactive, and quiet spread goes unseen.

📈 With wastewater

The whole community is read at once — including people who never get tested — giving 1–4 weeks of lead time to act.

Earlier: signal precedes hospital load Complete: includes untested & asymptomatic Cheaper: 5 sites cover 12.94 lakh people
How it works

Test the sewage, not the people.

Wastewater from every home flows to the city's treatment plants. We read it. Press play and watch one sample travel from the sewer to an early-warning alert.

What you get: outbreak early warning · NCD & nutrition trends · sector hotspot maps
What we see

Two signals from one sample.

The same wastewater carries both the city's infectious threats and the slow-moving trends behind chronic disease.

Viral & pathogen

Early warning for outbreaks

Catch waterborne and seasonal threats rising weeks before clinics do.

  • Typhoid — waterborne; a spike flags contaminated supply
  • Hepatitis A — food & water spread; sheds before symptoms
  • Dengue — post-monsoon surge; times vector control
  • COVID / Flu / RSV — respiratory load, independent of testing
  • + Cholera and more
NCD & lifestyle

Chronic-disease & lifestyle trends

A population read on the habits behind India's biggest health burden.

  • Alcohol — community use trend for de-addiction policy
  • Tobacco — nicotine marker targets cessation outreach
  • Diet quality — nutrition read for school-meal & policy
  • Diabetes & cardio — drug-metabolite proxy for chronic load
  • + more markers as needed

Privacy by design

Population-level only. Fully anonymous. DPDP Act 2023-aligned.

Pooled samples

Wastewater mixes thousands of people. No individual can ever be identified or singled out.

Aggregation threshold

Results are reported only as sector-level aggregates above a 10,000-population threshold.

No personal data

Nothing is collected from any person — no names, no consent burden, no individual tracking.

Impact

What officials do with the signal.

Outbreak early-warning

A rising typhoid or hepatitis signal in a sector triggers water-quality checks before cases climb.

NCD & nutrition policy

Alcohol, tobacco and diet trends guide where to place screening camps and awareness drives.

Mass-gathering safety

During melas and large events, daily sampling watches for any rise in waterborne risk.

Resource targeting

Limited staff, kits and vaccines go to the sectors that the data shows need them first.
